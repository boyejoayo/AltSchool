Nigerian Jollof Rice

A vibrant and flavourful West-African classic that is moist, spicy rice simmered in a rich tomato-pepper sauce, often served with fried chicken or beef, plantains, or salad. This authentic jollof is hearty, satisfying, and perfect for sharing!

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: approx. 30 minutes
  • Cook Time: approx. 1 hour (including simmering)
  • Servings: ~8 people
  • Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of rice
  • 1 kg meat (chicken or beef)
  • 1000 ml ground fresh tomatoes
  • 50 ml ground fresh peppers
  • 2 cups sliced onions
  • 300 ml vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 “fingers” of fresh ginger (grated or ground)
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp nutmeg powder
  • 2–3 seasoning cubes (or to taste)
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Season the meat with salt, one seasoning cube, half the curry and thyme, and half the sliced onions. Let it sit for a few minutes.
  2. Boil the meat until tender (about 10–15 min for chicken; longer if beef). Save the stock when done.
  3. Peel the garlic and ginger; grind or pound them together and set aside.
  4. Parboil the rice: boil in water for about 3 minutes, then drain and rinse thoroughly. Set aside.
  5. Deep-fry the cooked meat until crisp; set aside.
  6. In the same oil (about 300 ml initially), sauté the remaining onions 1–2 min, then add the ground tomato + pepper blend. Fry for ~20 min, stirring frequently until the tomato darkens and oil starts to rise to the top.
  7. Add the meat stock, the ground ginger/garlic, remaining curry & thyme, nutmeg, salt to taste, another seasoning cube, and about 4 cups of water. Stir and taste such that the mixture should be heavily spiced (the rice will balance it).
  8. Add the parboiled rice, stir to combine, then cover and reduce heat to low. Cook 30–45 min, stirring occasionally, adding water if needed so rice doesn’t burn or stick.
  9. Once rice is cooked through, fluff gently, then serve hot with the fried meat. Enjoy with plantains, salad or vegetables if desired.

Tips & Notes

For extra flavor, allow the bottom layer to caramelize slightly to give the signature smoky “party jollof” taste. If the tomato sauce threatens to burn before rice is added, remove a portion of it and re-add later. You can also adjust the heat by reducing the amount of fresh pepper, or substituting with milder peppers.

Nutrition Facts

Per serving: ~540 kcal, but values vary depending on meat, oil amount, and portion size.
